Chili

Tim's Lazy Chili Recipe (vegeterian)

Why cook this?

It's delicious. Easy to cook, as in takes minimal culinary skills into play.

Cheap! Can be stored in a fridge for a week. And on day 3-4 it tastes the best!

Best consumed with rice, due to beans lacking all 9 essential amino acids.

What tools you will need

A pot or a skillet with high walls for Chili and another for rice, chopping board, something to stir with

and something to put food on a plate from the pot. A plate, but not neccessary ;) And a kettle.

Ingredients

First, need to select the beans: white - better for beginners, red/black - not for amateurs.

Beans need to be soaked for some time (4-12 hours), so plan ahead!

As for other ingredients, they depend on the region you live in, so buy what is freshest.

    The list:
  • onions (preferably white)
  • carrots
  • tomatoes (red)
  • bell peppers (red)
  • chili flakes or fresh chilis (depends on your heat tolerance)
  • spices (corriander, basil, thyme, black pepper)
  • lime or lemon
  • oil with high smoking point
  • salt TT

Let's get cooking!

Before you start: beans to an inexperienced "user" are to be taken with caution, cuz of the GAS...

If that's the case, then get rid of the water they were soaked in, bring them to boil and drain once more.

    Mise en Place:

  1. 1-2 hours | cook the beans until 8/10 ready
  2. roughly cut carrots, onions, bell peppers, tomatoes
  3. prepare hot water in kettle

    Preparation process:

  1. stirfry carrots and onions until golden
  2. start the rice, assumin it takes 15-20 minutes
  3. add bell peppers, tomatoes and spices, high heat 5-10 mins
  4. add beans and fry a bit more to add more taste
  5. pour boiling water to just cover the beans
  6. let simmer on low heat and closed lid for 20-30 minutes or until the beans are ready

ENJOY!
